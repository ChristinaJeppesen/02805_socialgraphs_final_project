He played college basketball for [[Memphis Tigers men's basketball|Memphis]], where he was named the [[Conference USA Men's Basketball Player of the Year]] in 2012. He was selected 40th overall in the [[2012 NBA draft]] by the [[Portland Trail Blazers]] and played for the [[Idaho Stampede]] of the [[NBA G League]] before being traded to the [[Denver Nuggets]] in 2015, where he eventually became their franchise leader in three-pointers made. He was traded to the [[Washington Wizards]] in 2022.\n\n==High school career==\nBarton, the No. 6-rated player in the nation in 2010, attended four schools in five years. He started his basketball career at [[Baltimore City College Basketball|Baltimore City College]], a public college preparatory high school, for two years before reclassing and transferring to National Christian Academy in [[Fort Washington, Maryland]] to repeat his sophomore year.  Barton then enrolled at Baltimore's [[Lake Clifton Eastern High School]] for his junior year. decided to forgo his final two years of eligibility and declare for the [[2012 NBA draft]].<ref>[http://espn.go.com/nba/draft2012/story/_/id/7723474/memphis-tigers-guard-barton-declare-draft-source-says Will Barton to declare for draft]</ref> He was selected by the [[Portland Trail Blazers]] with the 40th overall pick.<ref>[http://www.oregonlive.com/blazers/index.ssf/2012/06/nba_draft_blazers_take_memphis_will_barton_with_no.html NBA draft: Blazers take Memphis' Will Barton with No. 40 pick, trade 41st selection to Brooklyn]</ref> On December 7, 2012, he was assigned to the [[Idaho Stampede]] of the [[NBA Development League]]. He was recalled on December 9, reassigned on January 6,<ref>[http://www.iamatrailblazersfan.com/ArticleDisplay/tabid/297/ItemID/3609/Default.aspx Nolan Smith, Will Barton Assigned to Idaho Stampede]</ref> and recalled again on January 9.<ref>[http://www.insidehoops.com/blog/?p=12024 Trail Blazers recall Nolan Smith and Will Barton from D-League]</ref> He made his first NBA start on April 10, 2013 against the [[Los Angeles Lakers]].\n\nOn February 26, 2014, Barton recorded a season-high 20 points and a career-high 11 rebounds in 124\u201380 win over the [[Brooklyn Nets]].<ref>[http://www.nba.com/games/20140226/BKNPOR/gameinfo.html Notebook: Trail Blazers 124, Nets 80]</ref> In a televised interview following the game, when asked by a reporter about a spontaneous \"Will Barton\" chant that had broken out late in the contest, the guard replied, \"I like to think I'm the people's champ,\" thereby instantly giving birth to a new nickname and a Trail Blazers [[meme]].<ref>{{cite web | last=Meagher | first=Sean | url=http://www.oregonlive.com/blazers/index.ssf/2014/02/will_barton_the_peoples_champ_help_lead_trail_blaz.html | title=Will Barton, the people's champ, helps lead Trail Blazers to 124-80 win over Nets (postgame video) | work=OregonLive.com | date=February 26, 2014 | access-date=December 29, 2014}}</ref> He went on to record 17 points and six rebounds on May 12 to help Portland win Game 4 of their semi-final match-up against the [[San Antonio Spurs]].\n\n===Denver Nuggets (2015\u20132022)===\n[[File:Will Barton (cropped).jpg|thumb|Barton with the Denver Nuggets in 2020]]\nOn February 19, 2015, Barton was traded, along with [[V\u00edctor Claver]], [[Thomas Robinson (basketball)|Thomas Robinson]] and a lottery-protected 2016 first-round pick, to the [[Denver Nuggets]] in exchange for [[Arron Afflalo]] and [[Alonzo Gee]].<ref>[http://www.nba.com/nuggets/news/nuggets-acquire-barton-claver-robinson-and-protected-first-round-pick-portland Nuggets Acquire Barton, Claver, Robinson and Protected First Round Pick From Portland]</ref>\n\nOn August 7, 2015, Barton re-signed with the Nuggets.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/nuggets/news/nuggets-re-sign-darrell-arthur-will-barton-and-jameer-nelson|title=Nuggets Re-Sign Darrell Arthur, Will Barton and Jameer Nelson|work=NBA.com|date=August 7, 2015|access-date=August 7, 2015}}</ref> On November 13, 2015, he scored a career-high 26 points in a 107\u201398 win over the Houston Rockets.<ref>[http://www.nba.com/games/20151113/HOUDEN/gameinfo.html Gallinari's scores 27 points, Nuggets top Rockets 107-98]</ref> He topped that mark on December 20 with 32 points against the New Orleans Pelicans.<ref>[http://www.nba.com/games/20151220/NOPDEN/gameinfo.html Davis leads Pelicans to 130-125 win over Nuggets]</ref> Barton's strong pre-Christmas play earned him recognition as a possible contender for the 2015\u201316 Sixth Man of the Year award.<ref>[http://www.basketballinsiders.com/nba-am-barton-making-case-for-sixth-man-honors/ NBA AM: Barton Making Case For Sixth Man Honors]</ref> Over the Nuggets' first 29 games of the season, Barton averaged 15.7 points, 6.1 rebounds, 2.3 assists and 1.1 steals in 29.0 minutes per game off the bench.<ref>[https://www.basketball-reference.com/players/b/bartowi01/gamelog/2016/ Will Barton 2015-16 Game Log]</ref>\n\nBarton appeared in just six of the Nuggets' first 18 games of the 2016\u201317 season. He played in the first three games before missing the next nine after spraining his left ankle. He then returned for three before missing another for personal reasons, and then a further two with another left ankle complaint.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/nba/recap?gameId=400899723|title=Whiteside's double-double lifts Heat over Nuggets 106-98|work=ESPN.com|date=November 30, 2016|access-date=December 1, 2016}}</ref> On January 17, 2017, he had season highs of 26 points and eight assists in a 127\u2013121 win over the [[Los Angeles Lakers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/nba/recap?gameId=400900047|title=Jokic scores 29, Nuggets hold off struggling Lakers 127-121|work=ESPN.com|date=January 17, 2017|access-date=January 18, 2017}}</ref> On February 6, 2017, he scored a then season-high 31 points and hit five three-pointers in a 110\u201387 win over the [[Dallas Mavericks]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/nba/recap?gameId=400900181|title=Barton scores 31 in Nuggets' 110-87 win over Mavericks|work=ESPN.com|date=February 6, 2017|access-date=February 7, 2017}}</ref> On March 13, 2017, in a 129\u2013101 win over the [[Los Angeles Lakers]], Barton scored 22 points and went 3 of 4 behind the arc and 6 of 9 overall in his most productive outing since the All-Star break. Prior to the game against the Lakers, Barton had made just five of his previous 21 three-point tries.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/nba/recap?gameId=400900393|title=Barton, Murray each score
