The next year he appeared in ''[[Working Girl]]'' (1988). He had a small recurring role as [[Denise Bryson]], a [[transgender]] [[Drug Enforcement Administration|DEA]] agent on the series ''[[Twin Peaks]]'' and played the narrator and host in the [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]] softcore erotica TV series ''[[Red Shoe Diaries]]''. In 1992, he played the role of [[Roland Totheroh|Rollie Totheroh]] in the biographic film ''[[Chaplin (film)|Chaplin]]'', directed by [[Richard Attenborough]] and based on the life of [[Charlie Chaplin]]. In 1992 he had a small role in the family film ''[[Beethoven (film)|Beethoven]]''.\n\n[[File:David Duchovny 2011 Shankbone.JPG|thumb|upright|Duchovny at the [[Tribeca Film Festival]] in April 2011]]\n\nIn 1993, Duchovny began starring in the [[science fiction]] series ''[[The X-Files]]'', as FBI Special Agent [[Fox Mulder]], a [[conspiracy theorist]] who believed his sister had been abducted by [[Extraterrestrial life in popular culture|aliens]].<ref name=\"actors\"/> The show evolved into a cult hit and became one of [[Fox Broadcasting Company|The Fox Network]]'s first major television hits. According to ''The X-Files'' creator Chris Carter, Duchovny turned out to be one of the best-read people he knew.<ref>{{cite web |last= Carter |first= Chris |url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/cult/xfiles/interviews/carter/clip0.shtml |title=Casting Mulder |work=[[BBC News]] |access-date=July 12, 2009}}</ref> After getting the role, Duchovny thought the show would not last for long or make as much impact as it did. [[Executive producer]] [[Frank Spotnitz]] called Duchovny \"amazingly smart\". Spotnitz further stated that Duchovny was behind some of the main characteristic ideas behind Mulder.<ref>{{cite web |last= Spotnitz |first= Frank |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/cult/xfiles/interviews/spotnitz/page6.shtml |title=Amazingly Smart |work=[[BBC News]]|access-date=July 12, 2009}}</ref> Also in 1993, Duchovny was cast alongside [[Brad Pitt]] and [[Juliette Lewis]], in the [[Dominic Sena]]-directed thriller ''[[Kalifornia]]''.\n\nDuring ''[[The X-Files]]'' run, in between the fifth and sixth seasons, Duchovny co-starred alongside [[Gillian Anderson]] in a 1998 film also titled ''[[The X-Files (film)|The X-Files]]'' that continued the storyline.<ref name=\"actors\"/> He remained with the series until leaving the show in 2001, partly because of a contract dispute that occurred after season seven finished filming.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/1337685.stm |date= May 18, 2001 |title= Duchovny quits X-Files |work= [[BBC News]] | access-date= March 19, 2015}}</ref> Duchovny appeared in half of the season eight episodes, but did not appear in season nine until the series finale in 2002. He also provided the voice for a parody of his Mulder character in the episode \"[[The Springfield Files]]\" of the animated comedy series ''[[The Simpsons]]''. Duchovny has been nominated for four [[Emmy Awards]].<ref>[http://www.emmys.com/celebrities/david-duchovny David Duchovny] at Emmys.com</ref>\n\nDuchovny caused controversy when it became public that he was the primary reason for ''The X-Files'' moving filming locations from [[Vancouver]], [[British Columbia]] to Los Angeles in 1998. Many residents of Vancouver were upset with Duchovny over scripted jokes on ''[[Late Night with Conan O'Brien]]'' about the city's heavy rainfall, including one where he joked, \"Vancouver is a very nice place if you like 400 inches of rainfall a day.\" (Duchovny's character Mulder would later reference this joke in the Season 5 episode \"[[Schizogeny (The X-Files)|Schizogeny]].\") He also stated, \"Of course, I'm tired of the rain. But if I wasn't married to a woman that lives in L.A., I'd stay in Vancouver.
