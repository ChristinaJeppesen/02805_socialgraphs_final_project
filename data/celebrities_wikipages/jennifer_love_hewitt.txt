Hewitt began her career as a child actress and singer, appearing in national television commercials before joining the cast of the [[Disney Channel]] series ''[[Kids Incorporated]]'' (1989\u20131991). She had her breakthrough as Sarah Reeves Merrin on the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] teen drama ''[[Party of Five]]'' (1995\u20131999) and rose to fame as a teen star for her role as Julie James in the horror films ''[[I Know What You Did Last Summer]]'' (1997) and its [[I Still Know What You Did Last Summer|1998 sequel]], as well as her role as Amanda Beckett in the teen comedy film ''[[Can't Hardly Wait]]'' (1998).\n\nHewitt's other notable films include ''[[Heartbreakers (2001 film)|Heartbreakers]]'' (2001), ''[[The Tuxedo]]'' (2002) and the two [[Garfield: The Movie|''Garfield'' live-action films]] (2004\u20132006). She has starred as [[Melinda Gordon]] on the [[CBS]] supernatural drama ''[[Ghost Whisperer]]'' (2005\u20132010), Riley Parks on the [[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]] drama series ''[[The Client List (TV series)|The Client List]]'' (2012\u20132013), Special Agent Kate Callahan on the CBS crime drama ''[[Criminal Minds]]'' (2014\u20132015), and since 2018, Maddie Buckley on the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] first-responder procedural {{Nowrap|''[[9-1-1 (TV series)|9-1-1]]''}}. She was nominated for the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress \u2013 Miniseries or Television Film]] for [[The Client List|''The Client List'' pilot film]] (2010).\n\nIn music, Hewitt has released four studio albums to date. After her debut album, ''[[Love Songs (Jennifer Love Hewitt album)|Love Songs]]'' (1992), was released at age 12 exclusively in [[Japan]], she went on to record ''[[Let's Go Bang]]'' (1995), ''[[Jennifer Love Hewitt (album)|Jennifer Love Hewitt]]'' (1996) and ''[[BareNaked]]'' (2002), the latter of which became her first album to chart in the United States, peaking at number 37 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.
