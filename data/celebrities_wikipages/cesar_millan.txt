The show consists of one-half lecture and one-half demonstration with local shelter dogs, in which he uses his [[Pack (canine)|pack]]-leader training techniques to modify negative behaviors.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.denverpost.com/2014/03/21/cesar-millan-hits-denver-to-train-humans-on-his-dog-centric-live-tour/ |title=Cesar Millan hits Denver \u2014 to train humans \u2014 on his dog-centric live tour |date=2014-03-21 |work=The Denver Post |access-date=2018-03-26 |language=en-US}}</ref>\n\n===Dog training technique===\nMillan's work focuses on handling a dog with what he calls \"calm-assertive energy\".<ref name=\"NYTceo\"/> He believes that dog owners should establish their role as calm-assertive pack leaders.<ref name=\"allheal\"/> According to Millan, dogs have three primary needs:<ref name=\"allheal\"/> exercise, discipline and affection, in that order.<ref name=\"Johnson, Morieka V\">{{cite web | url=http://www.azcentral.com/families/articles/0404dog-whisperer04-ON.html | title=Dog's best friend | author=Johnson, Morieka V. | date=April 4, 2006 | publisher=AZ Central | access-date=January 8, 2010}}</ref> In other words, it is the owner's responsibility to fulfill the dog's energy level needs through challenging exercise; then to provide clearly communicated rules, boundaries and limitations; and finally, to provide affection.<ref name=\"abc\">{{cite web | title = The Dog Whisperer's Magic Touch | publisher = ABC, NHancy Weiner, July 31, 2006 | url = https://abcnews.go.com/Nightline/story?id=2253861&page=1}}</ref> \n\nAccording to Millan, a common pitfall for American dog owners is to give a great deal of affection with very little exercise and even less discipline.<ref name=\"abc\"/> He encourages owners to understand the effect their own attitudes, internal emotions and physical postures have on a dog's behavior, counseling owners to hold strong posture (i.e., shoulders high and chest forward) and to project energy that is calm-assertive.<ref name=\"Gladwell\"/><ref name=\"bg3\"/>\n\nMillan's TV programs are centered on the rehabilitation of dogs while Millan concurrently educates the dog owners in his dog-handling philosophy. Conversations with owners typically revolve around his philosophy: that healthy, balanced dogs require strong \"pack leadership\" from their owners,<ref name=\"allheal\"/> while Millan demonstrates how owners can achieve and maintain a leadership role with their dogs.\n\nIn some cases, Millan takes dogs with severe behavioral problems to his Dog Psychology Center for an extended period of more intensive rehabilitation. The programs are not intended as a dog training guide, and<ref name=\"usatoday\" /> each episode contains repeated warnings that viewers should not try the behavior modification techniques at home without the guidance of a professional.<ref name=\"allheal\" />\n\nWhile working with a dog, Millan often uses vocal marks such as ''tsch'',<ref name=\"how to tsch\">{{cite web | title = How to 'Tsch!'
