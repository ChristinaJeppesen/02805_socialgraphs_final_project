He began his teaching career in the 1970s working at St. Lucy's School in [[Spanish Harlem]]. In 1977, he took a teaching position at [[La Roche College]] in [[McCandless, Pennsylvania]]. In 1980, he received his doctorate in [[sociology]] from [[New York University]].<ref name=\"bio\">{{Cite web|last=Bill|title=About Us|url=https://www.catholicleague.org/about-us/|access-date=2020-09-29|website=Catholic League|date=January 18, 2011|language=en-US}}</ref> Donohue is divorced and has two adult children from his marriage.<ref name=\"Marine\">{{cite news |title=A 'Marine' for Catholics Sees a Time of Battle  |first=Paul |last=Vitello |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/15/nyregion/15donohue.html |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |date=May 14, 2009 |access-date= March 27, 2014}}</ref>\n\nHis first book was ''The Politics of the American Civil Liberties Union'' and he became associated with the conservative [[Heritage Foundation]], for which he is an adjunct scholar.\n\nWhile Donohue was in college in New York, [[Virgil Blum|Virgil C. Blum]], a Jesuit at [[Marquette University]] in [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]], founded the Catholic League to counter [[anti-Catholicism]] in [[Culture of the United States|American culture]]. Blum died in 1990; in 1993, Donohue became the director of the organization.{{Citation needed|date=April 2010}}\n\nDonohue publishes ''The Catalyst'', the Catholic League journal. He formerly served on the board of directors of the [[National Association of Scholars]].<ref name=\"NAS_contact\">{{cite web |url=http://www.nas.org/contact.cfm |title=National Association of Scholars: Contact Us |access-date=November 23, 2009 |year=2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091205232218/http://www.nas.org/contact.cfm |archive-date=December 5, 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He serves on the board of advisers of the [[Washington Legal Foundation]], the [[Howard Center for Family, Religion and Society]], the Society of Catholic Social Scientists, Catholics United for the Faith, the Ave Maria Institute, the Christian Film and Television Commission and [[Catholic War Veterans]].
