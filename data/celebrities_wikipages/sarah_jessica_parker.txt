Parker held the role for a year. In 1982, Parker obtained the lead role of the [[CBS]] sitcom ''[[Square Pegs]]'', which lasted just one season. Her performance as a shy teen who showed hidden depths, nevertheless, was acclaimed by critics. In the three years that followed, Parker was cast in four films: the most significant being ''[[Footloose (1984 film)|Footloose]]'' (1984) and ''[[Girls Just Want to Have Fun (film)|Girls Just Want to Have Fun]]'' (1985), with [[Helen Hunt]]. Set in New York City and based on [[Candace Bushnell]]'s [[Sex and the City (book)|1997 book of the same name]], the show follows the lives of a group of four women\u2014three in their mid-thirties and one in her forties\u2014who, despite their different natures and ever-changing sex lives, remain inseparable and confide in each other. Her role was that of [[Carrie Bradshaw]], the narrator and main protagonist, with each episode structured around her train of thought while writing her weekly column \"Sex and the City\" for the fictitious paper, the ''New York Star''. Response towards her performance was positive during the show's run and in the years immediately thereafter.
