3\n|updated            = November 18, 2018\n}}\n}}\n'''Danica Sue Patrick''' ({{IPAc-en|\u02c8|d|\u00e6|n|\u026a|k|\u0259}}; born March 25, 1982) is an American former professional racing driver. She is the most successful woman in the history of [[American open-wheel car racing]]\u2014her victory in the [[2008 Indy Japan 300]] is the only win by a woman in an [[IndyCar Series]] race.\n\nBorn to a working-class family in [[Beloit, Wisconsin]], Patrick began [[Kart racing|karting]] at the age of ten and achieved early success by winning her class in the [[World Karting Association|World Karting Association Grand National Championship]] three times in the mid-1990s. She [[High school dropouts|dropped out of high school]] with her parents' permission in 1998, and moved to the United Kingdom to further her career. Patrick competed in Formula Vauxhall and [[Formula Ford]] before returning to the United States in 2001 due to a lack of funding. In [[2002 Barber Dodge Pro Series|2002]], she competed in five [[Barber Pro Series|Barber Dodge Pro Series]] races for [[Rahal Letterman Lanigan Racing|Rahal Letterman Racing]]. Patrick later raced in the [[Atlantic Championship|Toyota Atlantic Series]] for the next two years. Her best effort was third in the championship standings for the [[2004 Atlantic Championship|2004 season]] where she became the first woman to win a [[pole position]] in the series.\n\nShe first drove in the IndyCar Series with [[Rahal Letterman Lanigan Racing|Rahal Letterman Racing]] in [[2005 IndyCar Series|2005]] and took three [[pole position]]s, equaling [[Tomas Scheckter]]'s record of poles in a rookie season. She was named the [[Indianapolis 500 Rookie of the Year|Rookie of the Year]] for both the [[2005 Indianapolis 500]] and the [[List of American Championship Car Rookie of the Year Winners|2005 IndyCar Series]]. She improved over the next two years with Rahal Letterman Racing in [[2006 IndyCar Series|2006]] and later [[Andretti Autosport|Andretti Green Racing]] in [[2007 IndyCar Series|2007]]. In [[2008 IndyCar Series|2008]], Patrick followed up her Japan victory to place sixth overall in the drivers' standings. She improved on this to secure fifth the following season, which saw her finish a career-high third at the [[2009 Indianapolis 500|Indianapolis 500]], the best performance by any woman at the race. Patrick's overall form declined during [[2010 IndyCar Series|2010]], but she still managed two-second-places at [[Oval track racing|oval tracks]] before leaving IndyCar after the [[2011 IndyCar Series|2011 season]] to focus on [[stock car racing]] full-time.\n\nPatrick began racing stock cars in 2010 in the [[NASCAR Xfinity Series|NASCAR Nationwide Series]] (now Xfinity Series) with her best result coming in the form of a fourth-place finish at [[Las Vegas Motor Speedway]] in [[2011 NASCAR Nationwide Series|2011]]. She placed a career-high tenth in the [[2012 NASCAR Nationwide Series|2012 season]] standings and was the second woman to clinch a pole position in the Nationwide Series after [[Shawna Robinson]] in [[1994 NASCAR Busch Series|1994]]. Patrick started in the Sprint Cup Series (now NASCAR Cup Series) in 2012. She became the first woman to win a Cup Series pole position by setting the fastest qualifying lap for the [[2013 Daytona 500]], finishing eighth. Patrick bested [[Janet Guthrie]]'s record for the most top-ten finishes by a woman in the Sprint Cup Series in [[2015 NASCAR Sprint Cup Series|2015]].
