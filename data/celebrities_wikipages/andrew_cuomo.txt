He began his career working as the campaign manager for his father in the [[1982 New York gubernatorial election]]. Later, Cuomo worked as an assistant district attorney in New York City, entered the private practice of law, founded a housing non-profit, and chaired the New York City Homeless Commission from 1990 to 1993. Cuomo served as [[United States Department of Housing and Urban Development|assistant secretary of Housing and Urban Development]] from 1993 to 1997. From 1997 to 2001, he served in President [[Bill Clinton]]'s [[Cabinet of the United States|Cabinet]] as the [[United States Secretary of Housing and Urban Development|11th United States secretary of housing and urban development]]. After failing to win the Democratic primary in the [[2002 New York gubernatorial election]], Cuomo was elected [[Attorney General of New York|New York attorney general]] in [[2006 New York Attorney General election|2006]].\n\nCuomo won the [[2010 New York gubernatorial election]] to become governor of New York. He was re-elected in [[2014 New York gubernatorial election|2014]] and [[2018 New York gubernatorial election|2018]] after winning primaries against progressive challengers. During his governorship, Cuomo signed the [[Marriage Equality Act (New York)|Marriage Equality Act]] in 2011 to legalize [[Same-sex marriage in New York|same-sex marriage]], the Compassionate Care Act in 2014 to [[Medical cannabis in the United States|legalize the medical use of cannabis]], and the [[Marijuana Regulation and Taxation Act]] in 2021 to [[Legalization of non-medical cannabis in the United States|legalize the recreational use of cannabis]]. Cuomo's administration oversaw the construction of the [[Governor Mario M.
