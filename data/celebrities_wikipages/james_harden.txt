professional [[basketball]] player for the [[Philadelphia 76ers]] of the [[National Basketball Association]] (NBA). Harden is regarded as one of the greatest scorers and [[Shooting guard|shooting guards]] in NBA history.<ref>{{cite web|last=Martin |first=Gus |url=https://fadeawayworld.net/nba/ranking-the-top-10-pure-scorers-in-nba-history |title=Ranking The Top 10 Pure Scorers In NBA History |publisher=Fadeaway World |date=2020-07-23 |accessdate=2022-05-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=2015\u201316 GM Survey|url=http://www.nba.com/news/features/john_schuhmann/nba-com-2015-16-gm-survey/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170306203301/http://www.nba.com/news/features/john_schuhmann/nba-com-2015-16-gm-survey/|archive-date=March 6, 2017|access-date=June 28, 2017|work=NBA.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Harden stands out among pack of top shooting guards|url=http://www.nba.com/2015/news/features/fran_blinebury/10/12/top-10-shooting-guards-for-2015-16/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170308221049/http://www.nba.com/2015/news/features/fran_blinebury/10/12/top-10-shooting-guards-for-2015-16/|archive-date=March 8, 2017|access-date=June 28, 2017|work=NBA.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Bailey|first=Andy|title=NBA All-Time Player Rankings: Top 10 Shooting Guards|url=https://bleacherreport.com/articles/2852997-nba-all-time-player-rankings-top-10-shooting-guards|access-date=2022-12-10|website=Bleacher Report|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hoopshype.com/lists/20-greatest-shooting-guards-ever-the-hoopshype-list/ |title=20 greatest shooting guards ever: The HoopsHype list |date=September 24, 2021 |publisher=hoopshype.com |access-date=December 10, 2022}}</ref>\n\nHe played [[college basketball]] for the [[Arizona State Sun Devils men's basketball|Arizona State Sun Devils]], where he was named a consensus [[NCAA Men's Basketball All-Americans|All-American]] and [[Pac-12 Conference Men's Basketball Player of the Year|Pac-10 Player of the Year]] in 2009. Harden was selected with the third overall pick in the [[2009 NBA draft]] by the [[Oklahoma City Thunder]]. In 2012, he was named [[NBA Sixth Man of the Year Award|NBA Sixth Man of the Year]] with the Thunder and helped the team reach the [[NBA Finals]], where they lost to the [[2011\u201312 Miami Heat season|Miami Heat]] in five games.\n\nUnhappy with the failure of the Oklahoma City Thunder to offer him a max contract, he refused their contract extension offer when it was substantially under the max. The Thunder subsequently traded him to the [[Houston Rockets]] before the 2012\u201313 season. In his first season with the team he set or matched several team records and was named to his first [[All-NBA Team]] (a third-team selection), as well as his first [[NBA All-Star]] team. Over the next seven and a half seasons with the team he would lead the league in scoring three times and assists once, and was named the [[NBA Most Valuable Player]] in {{nbay|2017|end}}. During his time in Houston, he was named to eight consecutive NBA All-Star teams and earned All-NBA Team honors seven times, including six first-team selections. In the beginning of the 2020\u201321 season, Harden was traded to the [[Brooklyn Nets]] as part of a four-team trade. With the Nets, he was named to his ninth and tenth consecutive All-Star games. In October 2021, Harden was honored as one of the league's greatest players of all time by being named to the [[NBA 75th Anniversary Team]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://nba.com/75 | title=My Finals Moment | website=[[NBA.com]] }}</ref>\n\nHarden is a two-time member of the [[United States men's national basketball team|United States national team]], winning gold medals at the [[Basketball at the 2012 Summer Olympics|2012 Summer Olympics]] and [[2014 FIBA Basketball World Cup|2014 FIBA World Cup]].\n\n==High school career==\nHarden attended [[Artesia High School (Lakewood, California)|Artesia High School]] in [[Lakewood, California]]. In his sophomore year, he averaged 13.2 points as Artesia went 28\u20135. He improved his stats to 18.8 points, 7.7 boards and 3.5 assists in his junior season and led Artesia to the California state title and a 33\u20131 record. Artesia repeated as state champions in Harden's final year after going 33\u20132. Harden had similar stats during the previous season: 18.8 points, 7.9 rebounds, and 3.9 assists. He was named a [[McDonald's All-American]], and also earned second-team [[Parade All-America Boys Basketball Team|''Parade'' All-American]] honors.\n\nHe also helped his [[Amateur Athletic Union|AAU]] team, Pump-N-Run Elite, to the 2006 Las Vegas Adidas Super 64 championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hsoregon.scout.com/a.z?s=201&p=2&c=550188 |title=Sorting Out A Zillion Players In Vegas |publisher=Hsoregon.scout.com |date=July 27, 2006 |access-date=June 28, 2017}}</ref> Harden had 34 points in the victory over a DC Assault team which included [[Michael Beasley]], [[Nolan Smith]] and [[Austin Freeman]]. In the game against Houston Hoops, played on the same day, Harden had 33 points.\nIn the final, Pump-N-Run Elite beat [[Kevin Love]]'s Southern California All-Stars.\n\n==College career==\nHarden's freshman year, [[Arizona State Sun Devils men's basketball|Arizona State]] was picked to finish ninth in the [[Pacific-10 Conference|Pac-10 Conference]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.pac-10.org/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/110107aad.html |title=UCLA Picked To Defend Pac-10 Title in 2007\u201308 |access-date=March 7, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090618015701/http://www.pac-10.org/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/110107aad.html |archive-date=June 18, 2009 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Behind his 17.8 points, 5.3 rebounds and 3.2 assists per game, the Sun Devils went 21\u201313 (9\u20139) and finished tied for fifth in the Pac-10.
