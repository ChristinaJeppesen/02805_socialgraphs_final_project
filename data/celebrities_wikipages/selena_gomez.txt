NO [[WP:ORIGINAL RESEARCH]]; [[MOS:BEGIN]], [[MOS:LEADBIO]]--> Gomez began her acting career on the children's television series ''[[Barney & Friends]]'' (2002\u20132004). As a teenager, she rose to prominence for starring as [[Alex Russo]] on the [[Disney Channel]] television series ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place]]'' (2007\u20132012). Alongside her television career, Gomez appeared in the films ''[[Another Cinderella Story]]'' (2008), ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place: The Movie]]'' (2009), ''[[Ramona and Beezus]]'' (2010), ''[[Monte Carlo (2011 film)|Monte Carlo]]'' (2011), ''[[Spring Breakers]]'' (2012), ''[[Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising]]'' (2016), and ''[[The Dead Don't Die (2019 film)|The Dead Don't Die]]'' (2019), and voiced [[Hotel Transylvania characters#Mavis|Mavis]] in the [[Hotel Transylvania|''Hotel Transylvania'' film franchise]] (2012\u20132022).\n\nGomez executive produced the [[Netflix]] television series ''[[13 Reasons Why]]'' (2017\u20132020) and ''[[Living Undocumented]]'' (2019) through her production company, July Moon Productions. Additionally, she executive produces and stars in the [[HBO Max]] cooking series ''[[Selena + Chef]]'' (2020\u2013present) and the [[Hulu]] mystery-comedy series ''[[Only Murders in the Building]]'' (2021\u2013present). The latter earned Gomez critical praise, and nominations for a [[Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Actress in a Comedy Series|Critics' Choice Television Award]], a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress \u2013 Television Series Musical or Comedy|Golden Globe Award]], and a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series|Primetime Emmy Award ]] (as producer).\n\nGomez released three albums with her former band, [[Selena Gomez & the Scene]], all of which reached the top ten of the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and were certified [[Music recording certification|gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA): ''[[Kiss & Tell (Selena Gomez & the Scene album)|Kiss & Tell]]'' (2009), ''[[A Year Without Rain]]'' (2010), and ''[[When the Sun Goes Down (Selena Gomez & the Scene album)|When the Sun Goes Down]]'' (2011). Her debut solo album, ''[[Stars Dance]]'' (2013), topped the U.S. ''Billboard'' 200, while its lead single, \"[[Come & Get It (Selena Gomez song)|Come & Get It]]\", reached the top ten in the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. Gomez's second studio album ''[[Revival (Selena Gomez album)|Revival]]'' (2015), debuted at number one in the US, was certified [[Music recording certification|platinum]] by the RIAA, and spawned three US top-ten entries: \"[[Good for You (song)|Good for You]]\", \"[[Same Old Love]]\", \"[[Hands to Myself]]\".  Her third studio album, ''[[Rare (Selena Gomez album)|Rare]]'' (2020), became her third consecutive number one in the US, and its lead single, \"[[Lose You to Love Me]]\", her first number-one song in the US and Canada. In 2021, she released the Spanish-language EP ''[[Revelaci\u00f3n]]'', for which she received a nomination for the [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album]].\n\nShe has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Selena Gomez|various accolades]] and was named ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'s}} [[Billboard Women in Music|Woman of the Year]] in 2017, and she also broke 15 [[Guinness World Records]]. ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine named her one of the [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]] in 2020. She has a large following on social media and is the [[List of most-followed Instagram accounts|most followed musician and actress]] on [[Instagram]]. Gomez's other ventures include makeup, clothing, handbag and fragrance lines. She has worked with many charitable organizations and served as a [[UNICEF]] ambassador since age 17.<ref>{{cite web|title=Charity, Action, Now!
