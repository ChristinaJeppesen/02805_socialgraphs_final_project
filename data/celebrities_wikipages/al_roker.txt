During his time in Oswego, he also DJ'd at the campus radio station, WNYO.<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=alroker|number=478235558138085376|title=I played AT40 at my college station WOCR 640AM SUNY Oswego 1973-1975|last=Roker|first=Al|date=2014-06-15|access-date=2019-09-12}}</ref> Following the completion of his studies, Roker moved to Washington, D.C. and took a weathercasting position at [[independent station (North America)|independent station]] [[WTTG]], then owned by [[Metromedia]], remaining there for much of the next two years.\n\nRoker's career with NBC began in 1978 when he was hired at [[WKYC]] in [[Cleveland]], then an NBC [[owned-and-operated station]]. After five years in Cleveland, Roker was promoted to the network's flagship outlet, [[WNBC-TV]] in his hometown.  Roker returned to New York City in late 1983 as a weekend weathercaster, and within eight months became the station's regular weeknight weathercaster.  Roker replaced 27-year WNBC-TV veteran [[Frank Field (meteorologist)|Dr. Frank Field]], who left the network over a contract dispute.  From 1983 to 1996, Roker was the regular substitute for forecaster [[Joe Witte]] on the NBC News program ''[[NBC News at Sunrise]]'', and from 1990 to 1995 filled in for [[Willard Scott]], [[Bryant Gumbel]] and 1997 through 2000's for [[Matt Lauer]] on the ''[[Today (NBC program)|Today Show]]''. In 1995, he became the host of ''The Al Roker Show'',<ref name=\"Food Network\">{{cite web |url=http://www.foodnetwork.com/al-roker/bio/index.html |title=Al Roker : Chefs |publisher=Food Network |access-date=2011-05-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110428034719/http://www.foodnetwork.com/al-roker/bio/index.html |archive-date=28 April 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> a weekend talk show on [[CNBC]].\n\nRoker received wider exposure when [[David Letterman]] asked him to join in an elevator race on an episode of the talk show ''[[Late Night with David Letterman]]'', which taped across the hall from the WNBC news studio in the [[GE Building]]. Roker conducted more interviews and segments on the show over time.
