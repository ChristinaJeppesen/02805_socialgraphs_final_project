Jeffries was a right-handed [[batsman (cricket)|batsman]] who bowled right-arm [[Seam bowling|medium pace]].  He was born in [[Oxford]], [[Oxfordshire]].\n\nJeffries made his debut for [[Oxfordshire County Cricket Club|Oxfordshire]] in the 1970 [[Minor Counties Championship]] against [[Dorset County Cricket Club|Dorset]].  Jeffries played [[Minor counties of English and Welsh cricket|Minor counties]] cricket for Oxfordshire from 1970 to 1980, which included 46 Minor Counties Championship matches.<ref>{{cite web |url-access=subscription  |url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/34/34693/Minor_Counties_Championship_Matches.html|title=Minor Counties Championship Matches played by Brian Jeffries|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=27 May 2011}}</ref>  He made his [[List A cricket|List A]] debut against [[Durham County Cricket Club|Durham]] in the [[1972 Gillette Cup]]. He first played for [[Minor Counties South]] in the [[1974 Benson & Hedges Cup]] against [[Glamorgan County Cricket Club|Glamorgan]].  Jeffries played 5 further List A matches for Minor Counties South, the last coming against [[Middlesex County Cricket Club|Middlesex]] in the [[1975 Benson & Hedges Cup]].<ref name = \"LAM\"/>  In addition, he played 2 List A matches for [[Minor Counties West]] in the [[1978 Benson & Hedges Cup]] against [[Derbyshire County Cricket Club|Derbyshire]] and [[Lancashire County Cricket Club|Lancashire]].<ref name = \"LAM\"/>  In total, Jeffries played 11 List A matches.  In these he scored 107 runs at a [[batting average (cricket)|batting average]] of 11.88, with a high score of 46.
