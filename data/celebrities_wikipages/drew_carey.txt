After serving in the [[United States Marine Corps|U.S. Marine Corps]] and making a name for himself in [[stand-up comedy]], he gained stardom in his own [[Situation comedy|sitcom]], ''[[The Drew Carey Show]]'', and as host of the U.S. version of the improv comedy show [[Whose Line Is It Anyway? He is a minority owner of the [[Major League Soccer]] team [[Seattle Sounders FC]], which won the MLS Cup, first in 2016 and again in 2019. He briefly participated in [[professional wrestling]], entering the [[Royal Rumble (2001)|2001 Royal Rumble]], and was inducted into the [[WWE Hall of Fame]] in 2011.\n\nCarey's autobiography, ''[[Dirty Jokes and Beer: Stories of the Unrefined]]'', details his early life and television career.\n\n==Early life and education==\n[[File:Drew Carey-Marines.jpg|alt=Head shot of a young, blond Carey dressed in military fatigues.|thumb|upright|Carey in his [[United States Marine Corps|U.S. Marine Corps]] uniform, with [[United States Marine Corps rank insignia|rank insignia]] of a [[Corporal#United States Marine Corps|Corporal]]]]\nCarey was born on May 23, 1958<ref>{{cite news|url=http://projects.latimes.com/hollywood/star-walk/drew-carey/|title=Drew Carey|last=Ferrell|first=David|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=February 11, 2010|access-date=October 17, 2018}}</ref> to Lewis and Beulah Carey.
